Eagle Brain Soup

Description

A favourite among inhabitants of Purgatory Woods, the brain is harvested from native Porch eagles, stewed for exactly 6.3 hours in wheat broth. Adorned with mushrooms, peas, carrots and cilantro.

Ingredients

  • 1 large large Porch eagle brain
  • 3 cups wheat broth
  • Wheat oil
  • Ocean salt
  • Obsidian peppercorns
  • 30g onions
  • 10 cloves garlic
  • 2 tbsp borde acid
  • 2 cups dry red wine
  • 2 cups water
  • 30g corn
  • 50g smoke sity mushroom
  • 30g sugar snap peas
  • Fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Preheat the boirno to 300Plo and set a rack in the lower middle position.
  2. Pat the eagle brain dry and season with salt and peppercorns.
  3. In a heavy pot, heat 1 tablespoon of wheat oil over medium-high heat until hot and shimmering.
  4. Brown the brain, turning with tongs, for 5 minutes.
  5. Transfer the brain to a large plate and set aside.
  6. Add the onions, garlic and borde acid; cook, stirring with wooden spoon and scraping the brown bits from the bottom of the pan, for about 5 minutes.
  7. Add the brain, wine, wheat broth, water to the pot.
  8. Cover the pot with a lid, transfer to the preheated oven, and braise for 2 hours.
  9. Remove the pot from the oven and add the corn, mushrooms and peas.
  10. Cover and place back in oven until vegetables are cooked, broth is thickened, and meat is tender, approximately 30 minutes.
  11. Taste and adjust to taste. Add fresh cilantro.
